The Loom of Logic

A Chronological Tapestry of Software Engineering.
Hover over the nodes to reveal the web of intellectual influence.

Ada Lovelace

1843 | The First Programmer

The concept of "Poetical Science" and universal computation.

"The Analytical Engine weaves algebraic patterns..."

Alan Turing

1936 | Theoretical Foundations

The "Universal Turing Machine" — the theoretical basis for all software.

Grace Hopper

1952 | First Compilers

The birth of compilers; the radical idea that code should be readable by humans.

Edsger Dijkstra

1968 | Structured Programming

The death of the "GOTO" statement. Brought mathematical rigor to programming.

Fred Brooks

1975 | The Mythical Man-Month

Establishing the socio-technical limits of software and law of diminishing returns.

Yourdon & Constantine

1979 | Structured Design

Invented Coupling and Cohesion (The ultimate metrics of modularity).

Gang of Four

1994 | Design Patterns

Standardizing architectural solutions into a common design vocabulary.

"Program to an interface, not an implementation."

Hunt & Thomas

1999 | The Pragmatic Programmer

The developer as a craftsman. Popularized the "Broken Window" theory in code.

Kent Beck

1999 | Extreme Programming

Pioneered Test-Driven Development (TDD) and the human-centric agile pivot.

Martin Fowler

1999 | Refactoring

The necessity of evolving code structure without changing its external behavior.

Mary & Tom Poppendieck

2003 | Lean Software

Applying the Toyota Production System to software. "Decide as late as possible."

Eric Evans

2004 | Domain-Driven Design

The Ubiquitous Language. Taught us that the code must map reality.

Jez Humble & David Farley

2010 | Continuous Delivery

The deployment pipeline as the central product of DevOps engineering.

Vaughn Vernon

2016 | Distilling DDD

Codifying DDD Bounded Contexts as the strict boundary lines for Microservices.

Nicole Forsgren

2018 | Accelerate & DORA

Proved mathematically what behaviors create high-performing technical teams.

Richards & Ford

2020 | Evolutionary Architecture

Using "Fitness Functions" to build automated architectural governance.
